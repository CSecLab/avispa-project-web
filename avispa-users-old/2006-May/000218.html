Hi Ilham,

> I have seen several protocol models in AVISPA library that use
> timestamp, such as Kerberos and APOP. In those models, timestamp is
> always assigned with new(). The user manual states new() as creating a
> nonce. However, the timestamp concept is not just about giving it some
> random value. At the very least, the random value should be a sequence
> number. 

you're right, the modeling of timestamps via nonces is not really
adequate. When having a closer look at the models, you will notice
that the "timestamps" are used as (not much more than) placeholders.
They get initialized, but if used at all, then for equality testing.

The problem is that HLPSL (and in particular the backends) do not
support time, and neither arithmetic. Counters can be simulated
to some extent using terms like "succ(succ(succ(zero)))", as done
e.g. for the tesla.hlpsl formalization. Maybe this little trick is
sufficient for your purposes, too.
